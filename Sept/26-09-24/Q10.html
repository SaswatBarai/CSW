S = V1 + 1/2at2n

The equation S = V1 + 1/2at2n describes the displacement (S) of an object under constant acceleration (a) from an initial velocity (V1).This equation is a modification of the more widely known equation D = V1 + 1/2at2n, which describes distance (D) instead of displacement.

Meaning of all variables:

  • V1: Initial velocity of the object at the starting point.
  • a: Constant acceleration applied to the object.
  • t: Time elapsed since the initial velocity
  • n: Not applicable in this equation, as it seems to be a mistake or an incomplete notation. The correct equation is S = V1 + 1/2at2n, without the ‘n’ term.
